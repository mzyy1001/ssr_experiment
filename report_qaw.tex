\textbf{Conclusions.}
\textbf{(1) Primary result}: SSR + LLM-dist at $w$=0.5 reaches Mean JS = \textbf{0.0257}, a \textbf{7.2\% relative reduction} over Direct SSR (0.0277).
\textbf{(2) Two signal sources}: (i) the LLM-dist mixture accounts for most of the gain; (ii) the SSR + multi-layer steering ensemble contributes an independent $\sim$5\% improvement.
\textbf{(3) Negative results to preserve}: single-layer steering at L24 (JS=0.0659), per-post LLM voting (JS=0.2360), persona-projected SSR, and deep multi-layer stacks all underperform the baseline.
\textbf{(4) Proposed paper framing}: SSR + LLM-dist as the main method; multi-layer steering ensemble as ablation; drop QAW, KL demographic reweighting, and the 23Q expansion — these were evaluated on synthetic questions and do not carry over to the real benchmark.
