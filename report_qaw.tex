\textbf{Conclusions.}
\textbf{(1)} Semantic relevance alone (QAW) is insufficient---temperature-based reweighting does not beat uniform (Tab.\,2--3).
\textbf{(2)} Demographic KL-penalty effectively corrects selection bias, winning on 65--70\% of questions (Tab.\,4--5).
\textbf{(3)} Combining KL-penalty with QAW achieves the best result: JS=0.0538, a 3.4\% relative reduction (Tab.\,6).
\textbf{(4)} LOO calibration selects identical hyperparameters across all 23 folds ($\tau$=0.1, KL\,str.=2.0), confirming cross-question transferability (Tab.\,7).
\textbf{(5)} Leave-$K$-out shows positive improvement for all $K$ from 1 to 11, demonstrating robustness (Tab.\,8).
