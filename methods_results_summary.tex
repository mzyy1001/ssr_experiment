\section{Setup}

\textbf{Benchmark:} 6 real survey questions on Chinese consumer health (TCM 藿香正气水):
8.0/qsingle\_3 (肠胃感冒季节), 8.0/qsingle\_4 (肠胃感冒场景), 9.0/qsingle\_3 (湿气原因), 9.0/qsingle\_4 (湿气症状), 9.0/qsingle\_5 (湿气缓解方法), 11.0/qsingle\_3 (中暑场景).

\textbf{Data:} $\sim$12K Weibo/Xiaohongshu posts → 66 latent topic clusters.
\textbf{Stack:} Qwen3-8B (open) + bge-base-zh-v1.5 embedder, single A100.
\textbf{Primary metric:} mean JS divergence over the 6 questions (lower = better).
\textbf{Secondary metrics:} $K_{xy}$ (KS-similarity, higher = better), $C_{xy}$ (cosine, higher = better) — for cross-paper diagnostic only (paper uses ordinal Likert; we have nominal multi-choice).

\textbf{Reference baseline:} Maier et al.\ 2026, \emph{LLMs Reproduce Human Purchase Intent via Semantic Similarity Elicitation of Likert Ratings}. Their SSR is \textbf{per-consumer + flat-aggregated} (eq.~1: $p_s(i)=\frac{1}{N_s}\sum_c \delta_{i r_c}$). \textbf{No clustering, no cluster-mass weighting.}

\textbf{Quarantined:} 23-question expanded eval (LLM-generated questions, not real surveys) is excluded from this report — see \texttt{results/quarantine\_23q/} and \texttt{experiments/quarantine\_23q/}.

\section{Method Families (Brief Catalog)}

Method families exhaustively tried, grouped by approach (each row in the next table corresponds to a family member):

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=1pt]
\item \textbf{Baselines}: Paper-SSR (Maier transfer, per-consumer flat); Direct SSR / M0 family (hierarchical cluster-weighted, sweep $n\_posts\in\{30,50,80,120,160\}$ and $top\text{-}K\in\{5,10,15,20\}$); persona-prompt baseline.
\item \textbf{LLM-distribution (M1)}: LLM-dist top-K (cluster-conditioned, $n\_samples\in\{3,5,10\}$); LLM per-post voting; SSR + LLM correction.
\item \textbf{Activation-steering (M3/M4)}: persona-vector extraction via CAA at $L\in\{8,12,16,20,24,28\}$; single-layer; multi-layer; ICL + steer; projected-persona SSR; steered M1.
\item \textbf{Ensembles (M2)}: M0+M1 weight sweep; SSR + multi-layer-steer; selective ensemble (KL-thresholded); SSR + LLM-dist; M2+M4 triple.
\item \textbf{Reweighting (alignment)}: default empirical; IS\_prov / IS\_age / IS\_prov+age; OT\_prov\_fast / OT\_prov\_sinkhorn; adaptive-$n$.
\item \textbf{QAW family} (Question-Adaptive Weighting + demographic-reweighting): no consistent gain on 6Q; quarantined under \texttt{quarantine\_23q/}.
\item \textbf{Controls}: random / shuffled / zero persona vectors as negative controls.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Key honest observations:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=0pt]
\item Q2 (gut-cold scenarios) drives $\sim$2/3 of the cross-baseline gain. Without Q2, M2 vs paper-SSR shrinks from $-40.9\%$ to $-16.9\%$.
\item M2 ensemble hurts Q3 ("湿气原因") by 50\% — likely because the LLM's distribution estimate disagrees strongly with SSR on this question, and naive averaging amplifies noise.

\item M1 (LLM-dist) wins on 3 of 6 questions individually but is unreliable (best on some, bad on Q3).
\end{itemize}

\section{Steering / Persona-Vector Story (Demoted to Appendix)}

\subsection{$\alpha \times L$ operating regime — pilot finding}

In an earlier 23Q exploration (now quarantined), persona-vector steering was \emph{conditionally} useful:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=0pt]
\item \textbf{Helps}: low $\alpha \le 0.3$ AND late layers $L \ge 24$ → up to $-21\%$ JS over unsteered.
\item \textbf{Direction-irrelevant zone}: $\alpha \ge 0.5$ middle layers → random and shuffled vectors match real ones (negative control passes — bad sign).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Negative controls}

Per-layer sweep with random / zero / shuffled persona vectors (\texttt{sweep.log}, \texttt{control\_\{random,shuffled,zero\}.json}): at aggressive $\alpha$, real vectors win only 2/6 to 4/6 questions vs zero — barely above chance, inconsistent across questions. Confirms steering does not robustly use persona-direction information at the operating regimes that improve mean JS.

\subsection{Why each failed method failed (one-liner)}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=0pt]
\item \textbf{LLM voting (per-post, JS=0.236)}: LLM votes the same answer regardless of post content; massive within-cluster collapse.
\item \textbf{SSR + LLM correction (JS=0.030)}: LLM rewrite adds noise to the SSR PMF.
\item \textbf{Projected-persona SSR (JS=0.04--0.06)}: projecting anchor embeddings along the persona vector destroys the anchor geometry that SSR depends on.
\item \textbf{QAW + demographic reweight}: no consistent gain on 6Q; quarantined under \texttt{quarantine\_23q/}.
\item \textbf{Selective ensemble (JS=0.030)}: KL-thresholded blending under-uses the LLM signal vs the simple linear ensemble.
\item \textbf{Steering at large $\alpha$ or many layers (JS=0.06--0.07)}: catastrophic — direction-irrelevant zone confirmed by random/shuffled controls matching real vectors.
\end{itemize}
